Contribution to Computer Science

I’ve been coding for over 2 years now and have learned some about modern technologies and practices. Between my schoolwork and professional responsibilities, writing code is a daily encounter.

In school I’ve learned the basics of computer architecture, operating systems, and algorithmic design. I’ve accomplished some projects to test my knowledge like building a mock CPU or developing a memory manager that deallocates/allocates blocks of memory for a user. Through these projects I have been able to hone my skills and become a better and more knowledgeable programmer. I have also learned the importance of collaborating with others and maximizing time.

As an intern at Lisam Systems I work on a variety of problems that clients and managers have. Most of the time I help provide modifications built to client specifications or help migrate data into our software solutions for onboarding clients. From time to time I also develop tools to retrieve regulatory information from different websites. To accomplish this, I use node.js and a headless browser to create web scrappers that gather the information and store it in a csv format or a Mongo database. These tools drastically simplify tasks that would take multiple people working weeks to accomplish. Each tool comes with its own set of challenges, but I am always happy with what I produce and how much I learn.

I am also passionate about teaching others what I know and inspiring them to pursue computer science. My position as an RA at Texas A&M puts me in contact with a lot of underclassmen that struggle with coding projects they must complete. I will often spend a few hours every week walking them through coding assignments and answering questions. I know how hard it can be starting out and want to make sure they receive the support they need to succeed.

Overall, I love solving problems with computers and I hope I can have more opportunities in the future to grow and accomplish greater things.
